Things I Believe
One
The model is never the bottleneck
In 12 years across two institutions, I never saw a strategy fail because the model was wrong. It always failed because the organization could not act on what the model said.
Two
Building from zero is the best education
I built analytics functions from scratch twice. There is no faster way to learn what matters than having nothing to inherit and everything to prove.
Three
Both sides need better information
Banks have all the data. Consumers have almost none of it. The information asymmetry hurts everyone. I am trying to close it, one essay at a time.
Four
Pricing is a decision system, not a spreadsheet
The institutions that win the next decade are the ones building adaptive, data-driven pricing infrastructure today. I know what that looks like because I built it.
