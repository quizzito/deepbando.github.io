Things I Believe
One
The model is never the bottleneck
In 12 years across two institutions, I never saw a strategy fail because the model was wrong. It always failed because the organization couldn’t act on what the model said. That gap is the real problem to solve.
Two
Building from zero is the best education
I built analytics functions from scratch twice — once rising through Citi, once as EQ Bank’s first hire. There’s no faster way to learn what actually matters than having nothing to inherit and everything to prove.
Three
Both sides of banking need better information
Banks have all the data. Consumers have almost none of it. Executives have the models but can’t always act on them. The information asymmetry hurts everyone. I’m trying to close it, one essay at a time.
Four
Pricing is a decision system, not a spreadsheet
Static pricing tables are a liability. The institutions that will win the next decade are the ones that build adaptive, data-driven pricing infrastructure today. I know what that looks like because I built it.
